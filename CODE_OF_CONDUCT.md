# Code of Conduct

BIOwerk is committed to providing a welcoming, safe, and inclusive environment for everyone. This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces—including GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, documentation, chat channels, and in-person or virtual events.

## Our Standards

Positive behaviors we encourage include:

- Demonstrating empathy, kindness, and respect toward others
- Being open to diverse viewpoints and experiences
- Giving and gracefully accepting constructive feedback
- Focusing on what is best for the community and the project
- Showing appreciation for contributions and collaboration

Behaviors that are unacceptable include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or derogatory comments (verbal, written, or visual)
- Public or private harassment, including unwelcome advances or sustained disruption
- Trolling, insults, or personal attacks
- Publishing others’ private information (doxing) without explicit permission
- Any conduct reasonably considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Responsibilities and Enforcement

- **Maintainers** are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct in good faith and will take corrective action when inappropriate behavior occurs.
- **Reporting**: If you observe or experience a violation, email `security@biowerk.example.com` or privately contact a maintainer. Include context, links, or evidence when possible.
- **Enforcement Guidelines** may include warnings, temporary bans, permanent bans, or escalation to appropriate authorities depending on severity and impact.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces and when an individual is representing the project or community in public spaces, including online forums, conferences, and social media.
